Hypersheet was a January-2016 experiment in "easy scalable hierarchical spreadsheets" - the hypothesis that spreadsheets should compose hierarchically (sheets within cells, nested groupings) rather than as flat grids. Small in scope (the model layer is essentially a single `Sheet`), but it's the earliest artifact of a hierarchical-data instinct that later resurfaced in z10 and karta.

Beyond the idea, the codebase was an early-adopter Relay/GraphQL build: forked directly off Facebook's Relay Starter Kit weeks after open-source release, with Relay still at 0.6 and GraphQL at 0.4. Full stack in a tiny repo - Express + express-graphql server, React 0.14 + fixed-data-table client, Sequelize over Postgres, and the Babel 6 / Webpack 1 pipeline of the era. Paused mid-flight rather than completed; archived as a historical reference point in the hierarchical-spreadsheet line of thinking.
