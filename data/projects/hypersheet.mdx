## About

A January-2016 experiment in hierarchical spreadsheets, built directly on Facebook's just-released Relay Starter Kit when Relay was still at 0.6 and GraphQL at 0.4. The product hypothesis — that spreadsheets should compose hierarchically rather than as flat grids — is the same instinct that later showed up in z10 and karta. Small in scope (the model layer is essentially a single `Sheet`), but interesting as the first artifact in that line of thinking and as an early-adopter Relay/GraphQL codebase.

## Why notable

- Earliest artifact of Amol's hierarchical-data thread — direct lineage to z10 and karta.
- Built on Relay 0.6 / GraphQL 0.4 in January 2016, weeks after they were open-sourced — willingness to adopt unstable bleeding-edge stacks.
- Demonstrates full-stack range in a tiny repo (GraphQL schema, Relay client, Sequelize/Postgres, Babel/Webpack pipeline).

## Architecture

- `server.js` — Express + `express-graphql` (Relay-style) entry point.
- `js/` — React 0.14 client (`app.js`, `components/`, `routes/`) using `fixed-data-table` for the grid surface and `material-ui` 0.14 for chrome.
- `data/schema.js` + `scripts/updateSchema.js` — GraphQL schema definition and the introspection-dump script Relay needed for build-time codegen.
- `models/sheet.js` + `migrations/` — Sequelize ORM over Postgres.
- Babel 6 transpile pipeline (`babel-preset-stage-0`/`es2015`/`react`), `webpack` 1.12 + `webpack-dev-server`.
