LifeSage was a venture to build an AI life-coaching product, a chat app where a "Sage" persona held long-running coaching conversations with a user. It reached alpha-1 - deployed at lifesage.ai on Render, LLC formed, analytics and IAP groundwork in place - before being paused. The Rails 7 + Hotwire stack carried disciplined fundamentals: ViewComponent, RSpec, Devise + Auth0, Tailwind/Haml templates, and a Person-centric domain where users and sages were both `Person` records linked through conversations and messages.

The more interesting part was the memory architecture. A family of `Derivative*` models captured asynchronously-computed summaries of conversations and person profiles, and every LLM call logged its token usage. That progressive-compaction approach - compressing long histories into retrievable artifacts - turned out to prefigure ideas I came back to years later in agent-callstack. Alpha-2 plans (vector-DB long-term memory, dynamic summary retrieval) were scoped but not shipped.
