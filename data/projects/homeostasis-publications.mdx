## About

The publications repo for Amol's independent theory-of-consciousness work. The central claim: phenomenal consciousness is not neurological, metaphysical, or informational — it is **physiological**, rooted in homeostasis. The manuscript builds the argument from the bottom up, starting at the mitochondrial proton gradient as a fully mechanistically describable system that nonetheless exhibits *embodied agency* (a "homeostatic force" toward a steady state). What it feels like to be such a system, the manuscript argues, is the visceral feeling of that force acting on it; experience is the felt transition of that force vector. The repo holds the in-progress manuscript plus the poster presented at ASSC22 (Association for the Scientific Study of Consciousness, 2022), which has a DOI.

## Why notable

- Original first-principles theory of phenomenal consciousness, argued from mitochondrial biophysics rather than neurological correlates — a position distinct from IIT, GWT, and predictive-processing accounts.
- Peer-presented at ASSC22, with a DOI assigned — not a private essay.
- Manuscript engages seriously with the homeostasis literature (Bernard, Cannon, Sterling on allostasis, Waddington, etc.) and reconciles competing terminology.
